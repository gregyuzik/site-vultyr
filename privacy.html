Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 27, 2026

Summary

vultyr does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data. Period.

Data Collection

vultyr does not collect any personal information. The app does not require an account, does not include any third-party tracking SDKs, and does not phone home to any server we operate.

Network Requests

The app makes direct HTTPS requests to public status page APIs (such as Statuspage.io, Apple, Google Cloud, and others) to check service status. These requests go directly from your device to the service's public API — they do not pass through any server we operate.

Data Storage

All data is stored locally on your device using Apple's SwiftData framework. If you enable iCloud Sync, your list of watched services is synced via Apple's iCloud Key-Value Store, which is governed by Apple's privacy policy. We never see this data.

Cross-Device Alerts

If you enable Cross-Device Alerts, status changes are shared between your devices via Apple's CloudKit. When your Mac detects a status change, it writes a record to your iCloud account's public database. Your iPhone receives a push notification via Apple's infrastructure. No third-party server is involved — all communication goes through Apple's CloudKit service. You can toggle this from any device.

Favicons

Service favicons are fetched from Google's public favicon service and cached locally on your device.

Website Analytics

This website (vultyr.app) uses Google Analytics to understand visitor traffic. The vultyr app itself does not include any analytics or tracking.

Third-Party Services

The vultyr app does not integrate with any third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking services. The only external requests are to public status APIs and Google's favicon service.

Children's Privacy

vultyr does not collect data from anyone, including children under 13.

Changes

If this policy changes, we will update the date above.
